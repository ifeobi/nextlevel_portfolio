# Google Ads Strategy — Ife Obijiofor Portfolio

## Country vs City Targeting

**Use city targeting.** You are selling a high-value B2B service, not a mass consumer product. The people who can actually hire you — founders, CTOs, ops-heavy business owners with real budget — cluster in specific cities. Country targeting bleeds spend into areas with no relevant decision-makers.

**Rule of thumb:**
- Country is fine when the country is effectively one metro (Netherlands → Amsterdam, Israel → Tel Aviv, Ireland → Dublin)
- City is mandatory for large countries (Germany, Australia, Canada, UAE)

**Exception:** Run country-level for 2–4 weeks first to collect impression data, then narrow to the top 2–3 cities by click quality before committing budget.

## Location Targeting — Underrated Markets

Ranked by opportunity-to-competition ratio for a Senior Fullstack + AI engineer with European work history.

---

### Tier 1 — Highest ROI, Least Saturated

**1. UAE / Dubai**
- Currency: AED (pegged to USD — effectively USD income)
- Tax-free
- Nigerian diaspora gives you warm cultural credibility
- Massive tech investment from Vision 2030 spilling over from Saudi Arabia
- Time zone gap: only 3 hours from Nigeria (UTC+4) — real-time calls are viable
- Decision-makers pay Western rates without flinching
- Best for: Fintech, ERP, SaaS clients, AI automation projects

**2. Netherlands / Belgium**
- Currency: EUR
- You have verifiable Belgian client work (Biggerocks BV, OnlyJewels BV) — unfair advantage
- Dutch and Belgian businesses trust you differently than any other candidate
- Dense SMB and startup markets with real budgets
- Best for: Both employment and freelance client acquisition

**3. Germany**
- Currency: EUR
- Massive Mittelstand (B2B mid-market firms) that are behind on digital and AI
- Strong demand for TypeScript/NestJS engineers specifically
- Less oversaturated with African talent compared to UK and US
- Best for: Full-time remote roles and ERP/automation projects

**4. Australia**
- Currency: AUD (~0.65 USD, but rates are competitive)
- Growing AI/SaaS scene, chronically short of senior engineers
- African engineers are underrepresented — you stand out
- Best for: Contract roles, AI-first startups

**5. Israel**
- Currency: ILS but most tech companies pay in USD
- One of the densest startup ecosystems per capita in the world
- Very AI-forward, high engineering standards
- Underrated as a target for African freelancers
- Best for: AI product companies, B2B SaaS

**6. Ireland**
- Currency: EUR
- Meta, Google, Stripe, Salesforce all have EU HQs here
- Remote-first culture well established
- Less competition than UK for Nigerian applicants
- Best for: Employment at multinational tech companies

**7. Canada**
- Currency: CAD (strong, stable)
- Heavy demand for AI/fullstack engineers
- Less competitive than US for Nigerian applicants
- Strong fintech and startup scene in Toronto and Vancouver
- Best for: Full-time remote roles

---

### Tier 2 — Good but More Competitive

- **UK** — GBP pays well but highly saturated with Nigerian devs already
- **Saudi Arabia** — massive tech budget but slower hiring cycle, better for employment search
- **Singapore** — strong fintech but far time zone and competitive APAC market
