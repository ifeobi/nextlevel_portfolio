# Copy Strategy: Sabri Suby Framework

## The Core Diagnosis

The original site committed the cardinal Suby sin: **the hero is Ife, not the buyer**.
Every Suby-compliant sales page makes the prospect the hero — you are the guide who
delivers their desired outcome. The old copy was a CV dressed up as a website.

Running Google Ads to a "Hi, I'm Ife" page is like paying to drive traffic to a
begging letter. This rewrite fixes that.

---

## The New Positioning

**Not:** "Open to remote opportunities" (desperate)
**Now:** "Currently evaluating 1–2 remote partnerships" (scarce, selective)

**Not:** A developer looking for work
**Now:** An engineer companies compete to hire — because the results speak for themselves

The prospect should feel: *"I need to reach out before someone else locks him in."*

---

## Sabri Suby Principles Applied

### 1. Lead With the Biggest Business Outcome
The $2M recovered receivables number is gold. It goes above the fold, in the hero,
in the About paragraph, and in the project card. Specificity = credibility.

### 2. The Hero Is the Buyer
Hero headline flipped from identity ("I'm Ife") to buyer benefit
("The engineer companies bring in when everything needs to ship").

### 3. Specificity Kills Skepticism
- 120s → 3s load time (not "improved performance")
- $2M+ in recovered receivables (not "business impact")
- 1,100+ TypeScript files (not "large codebase")
- 38+ AI tools (not "comprehensive AI system")

### 4. Scarcity Creates Urgency
Badge changed from availability signalling to scarcity signalling.
Contact section headed with "Limited availability."
About paragraph closes with evaluation framing, not job-seeking.

### 5. Make an Offer, Not a Request
Contact section reframed: not "reach out" but "work with me if you're the right fit."
The prospect feels they are applying to work *with* Ife, not Ife applying for their job.

### 6. Remove Anything That Destroys Trust
Fake testimonials (Acme Co, DEF Corp) were already not rendered — kept that way.
No placeholder social proof. Real metrics replace fake quotes.

### 7. Project Cards = Business Outcomes, Not Feature Lists
Every project card rewritten to lead with the business problem solved,
then the result, then the tech as proof of method.
